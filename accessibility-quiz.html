About Real Madrid CF

Real Madrid, founded in 1902, is one of the most successful football clubs in the world. The club's name was changed to Real Madrid in 1920, with the Spanish word "Real" meaning "royal" .

They've won a record 34 La Liga titles and 14 European Cup/Champions League titles. The 1950s and 60s were their most dominant periods, with the team winning five European Cups in a row .

In the 1980s, the team was led by the feared "La Quinta del Buitre" (The Vulture Cohort), which included players like Emilio Butragueño and Manolo Sanchís.

The 1990s saw a shift in power, with Barcelona dominating the league. However, Real Madrid won the Champions League in 1998 and 2000.

The 2000s saw the rise of the "Galacticos" era, with the team signing superstars like Luis Figo, Zinedine Zidane, and David Beckham.

Today, Real Madrid remains one of the biggest and most successful clubs in the world.
